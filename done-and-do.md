# Encounter Design

### Types of Encounters (Scenes)
In the *For Glory* system, gameplay is episodic and structured around different types of "Scenes." Encounters dictate the pacing, the mechanics used, and the narrative focus of the moment.

*   **Standard / Roleplay Scenes:** The party interacts with the environment or NPCs (such as negotiations or diplomacy), where the passage of time is usually of little consequence [1, 2]. 
*   **Fight Scenes:** Combat encounters that utilize Initiative, Attack, Evade, and the localized Wound/Suffering mechanics [1, 3, 4].
*   **Chase Scenes:** High-tension pursuits where participants are divided into hunters and quarry, rolling Agility checks to navigate discrete locations and overcome obstacles along a track [5, 6].
*   **Infiltration:** Stealth-focused encounters requiring planning and Speed checks to sneak into a location [2].
*   **Dramatic Tasks & Puzzles:** Encounters with a time constraint or trap where the correct course of action must be determined to pass an obstacle, sometimes utilizing a "Scene Die" to escalate tension [2, 7].
*   **Survival & Environmental Hazards:** Characters face natural disasters (earthquakes, wildfires), treacherous environments, or harsh conditions (starvation, extreme cold) that may reduce health or require rolls on specific Wound tables over time [2, 8, 9].
*   **Investigation:** Encounters focused on discovery, where clues are presented by the Patron [2].
*   **Montage Scenes:** Cinematic summaries of actions, which can include Travel Montages (with random trouble events) or Training Montages (where characters might gain abilities, scars, or gifts) [2, 10, 11].
*   **Flashbacks & Dream Sequences:** Narrative encounters used to reveal character backstory, motivations, or Patron-induced visions [2].
*   **Cut Scenes:** Purely narrative moments described by the Patron to fill in the story or cap off a previous scene [1].

### Encounter Neutral Event Content Types
For a neutral event table, you want elements that can introduce complications, boons, or weird flavor without necessarily forcing a combat state. These tables can contain a mix of good, bad, and item-based outcomes. **None of these content types are required for an encounter**, but they can be mixed and matched to create dynamic, emergent situations.

**1. Environmental Hazards & Anomalies**
*   *Mundane Obstacles:* Slippery floors, dead ends, deep muck, quicksand, crevices, or falling rocks [6, 12].
*   *Extreme Conditions:* Hot lava, erupting steam vents, corrosive environments, or high-pressure zones [12, 13].
*   *Anomalies & Weird Science:* Electromagnetic anomalies, psionic static fields, radiation, void storms, or localized gravity wells [12].
*   *Traps:* High-tech security, low-tech constructed traps, or magical/psionic snares [12].

**2. Neutral / Bizarre NPCs and Fauna**
*   *Crowds & Bystanders:* A protest march, a parade, a dog walker, elderly people, or workers moving a large piece of glass that the players must navigate around [12].
*   *Indifferent Beasts:* A family of giant, bear-sized housecats that will only attack if provoked [14, 15].
*   *Strange Merchants:* A bizarre shop that appears out of nowhere, such as a "Magic Shoppe" run by a creature who refuses gold and only barters for items with a history [16, 17].
*   *Cosmic / Fey Entities:* Witches brewing in a cauldron who ignore the party but will curse them if robbed, or tiny fairies that steal coins to feed to a living door [18-21].

**3. Loot, Items & Gear (Good & Cursed)**
*   *Mundane Kit:* Ropes, crowbars, caltrops, or magnifying glasses [22].
*   *Weird Pets & Familiars:* Finding a telepathic cat, a mechanical bird, a demon rat, or a flying eyeball in a jar [23, 24].
*   *Magical / High-Tech Baubles:* Items with highly specific uses, like Black Lotus Oil, invisible ink pens, thermoptic camouflage, or a bizarre puzzle box with unknown contents [25-27].
*   *Artifacts with a Catch:* A magical ring that demands the wearer steal an item every day, or a sword that hungers for pain and damages the wielder on a critical fumble [28, 29].

**4. Temporary Status Effects & Mutations**
*   *Fey Calamities & Curses:* A character's nose grows permanently long (granting +2 to smell but shock), their clothes fall off, or they are forced to speak only in rhyme [30-32].
*   *Alien / Sci-Fi Corruptions:* A strange energy arc that causes a random physical mutation (like an extra limb), forces the player to age rapidly, or downloads a new persona into their brain [33, 34].
*   *Poisons & Intoxicants:* A localized gas that causes coughing fits, sleep, dizziness, or hallucinations [16, 35].

**5. Narrative Triggers (Dooms & Boons)**
*   *Doom Activation:* An event specifically tailored to trigger a character's "Doom," such as a scenario forcing a claustrophobic character into a tight space, or tempting a kleptomaniac with an unguarded jewel [36].
*   *Sudden Escapes / Entrapment:* A cart of manure blocking the path, a bridge suddenly raising, or an unexpected ally opening a side door [12, 37].
